## 1. 十项设计原则

### 1.1 创作背景与演变

Rams 在 1970 年代末期，面对他称之为"形式、色彩和噪音的不可穿透的混乱"（"an impenetrable confusion of forms, colours and noises"）的周遭世界，开始反思自己的设计是否称得上"好设计"。这一自我诘问催生了十项原则。🏛️

> **关键时间线**：
> - **1972/1973**：Braun 设计总监 Dr. Fritz Eichler 首次撰写"十项设计命题"作为企业内部指南 [待核实一手原文] 🌐
> - **1976 年 12 月**：Rams 在纽约 Jack Lenor Larsen 展厅发表题为 "Design by Vitsœ" 的演讲，已包含十诫的大部分核心理念，但尚未格式化 🏛️
> - **late 1970s**：Rams 将原则系统化为十条，最初仅为 Braun 内部项目定义设计方法 🌐
> - **1993 年 4 月**：在 *Domus* 杂志第 748 期发表带详细阐释的版本 🏛️
> - **1995 年**：收入 *Weniger, aber besser* 一书正式出版 🏛️
> - **2002 年**：修订版，现为 Rams Foundation 官网使用的权威版本 🏛️

Rams 本人在 2023 年的 Capsule 杂志采访中澄清：

> "My ten principles are the quintessence of my own design practice — not a manifesto, but intentionally chosen principles that should be considered and discussed. ... My ten principles were originally part of an internal project at Braun, defining our approach to design. I expanded them over the years to include ten principles instead of six, since from the very beginning environmental soundness was important." 🏛️
> — Dieter Rams, Capsule Magazine 采访, 2023 年 4 月

在设计博物馆（Design Museum）的采访中，Rams 进一步说明写作动机：

> "Die habe ich zu Papier gebracht, weil Braun, je stärker der internationale Einfluss durch Gillette und andere Faktoren wurde, sich veränderte und ich etwas dagegen setzen wollte. Dass das dann irgendwo ausgewandert ist, sozusagen, das freut mich natürlich, aber war nicht die Absicht." 🏛️
> — Dieter Rams, Deutsches Design Museum 采访
>
> **译文**："我把它们写下来，是因为 Braun 在国际化影响（Gillette 等因素）下发生变化，我想对此做出抵抗。它们后来'出口'到世界各地，我当然感到高兴，但这并非初衷。"

**重要发现**：这不是 Rams 独自凭空创造的——设计史学者指出，Fritz Eichler 在 1972/1973 年已写出原始版本，Rams 在 1995 年进行了修改和发展。Rams 自己也强调这些原则"根植于手工艺传统"（handwerklichen Tradition），而非"典型德国式"设计态度。

### 1.2 完整原文

#### 英语版（2002 年修订版，Rams Foundation 官方文本）🏛️

> **Dieter Rams: Ten Principles for Good Design**
>
> I expressed in ten principles the basic considerations that determine my work as a designer, and which represent the main features of my design philosophy. However, they are not intended to be, nor can they be, regarded as incontrovertible, because the ideas of what constitutes good design continue to evolve – just as technology and culture also advance.
>
> **1. Good design is innovative.**
> The possibilities for innovation are not, by any means, exhausted. Technological development is always offering new opportunities for innovative design. But innovative design always develops in tandem with innovative technology, and can never be an end in itself.
>
> **2. Good design makes a product useful.**
> A product is bought to be used. It has to satisfy certain criteria, not only functional, but also psychological and aesthetic. Good design emphasises the usefulness of a product whilst disregarding anything that could possibly detract from it.
>
> **3. Good design is aesthetic.**
> The aesthetic quality of a product is integral to its usefulness because products we use every day affect our person and our well-being. But only well-executed objects can be beautiful.
>
> **4. Good design makes a product comprehensible.**
> It clarifies the product's structure. Better still, it can make the product talk. At best, it is self-explanatory.
>
> **5. Good design is unobtrusive.**
> Products fulfilling a purpose are like tools. They are neither decorative objects nor works of art. Their design should therefore be both neutral and restrained, to leave room for the user's self-expression.
>
> **6. Good design is honest.**
> It does not make a product more innovative, powerful or valuable than it really is. It does not attempt to manipulate the consumer with promises that cannot be kept.
>
> **7. Good design is enduring.**
> It avoids being fashionable and therefore never appears antiquated. Unlike fashionable design, it lasts many years – even in today's throwaway society.
>
> **8. Good design is consistent down to the last detail.**
> Nothing must be arbitrary or left to chance. Care and accuracy in the design process show respect towards the consumer.
>
> **9. Good design is environmentally friendly.**
> Design makes an important contribution to the preservation of the environment. It conserves resources and minimises physical and visual pollution throughout the lifecycle of the product.
>
> **10. Good design is as little design as possible.**
> Less, but better – because it concentrates on the essential aspects, and the products are not burdened with non-essentials. Back to purity, back to simplicity.
>
> Simplicity is the key to excellence.
>
> — 来源：rams-foundation.org

#### 德语版（2002 年修订版，Rams Foundation 官方文本）🏛️

> **Dieter Rams: 10 Thesen zum guten Design**
>
> Die Grundüberlegungen, die meine Arbeit als Designer bestimmen und die die Grundzüge meiner Design-Philosophie darstellen, wurden von mir in zehn Thesen formuliert. Eine unumstößliche Festschreibung sollen und können sie jedoch nicht sein, denn die Vorstellungen, was gutes Design ist, entwickeln sich weiter – so, wie sich Technik und Kultur weiterentwickeln.
>
> **1. Gutes Design ist innovativ.**
> Die Möglichkeiten für Innovation sind längst nicht ausgeschöpft. Die technologische Entwicklung bietet immer wieder neue Ausgangspunkte für zukunftsfähige Gestaltungskonzepte, die den Gebrauchswert eines Produktes optimieren. Dabei entsteht innovatives Design stets im Zusammenschluss mit innovativer Technik und ist niemals Selbstzweck.
>
> **2. Gutes Design macht ein Produkt brauchbar.**
> Man kauft ein Produkt, um es zu benutzen. Es soll bestimmte Funktionen erfüllen – Primärfunktionen ebenso wie ergänzende psychologische und ästhetische Funktionen. Gutes Design optimiert die Brauchbarkeit und lässt alles unberücksichtigt, was nicht diesem Ziel dient oder ihm gar entgegensteht.
>
> **3. Gutes Design ist ästhetisch.**
> Die ästhetische Qualität eines Produktes ist integraler Aspekt seiner Brauchbarkeit. Denn Geräte, die man täglich benutzt, prägen das persönliche Umfeld und beeinflussen das Wohlbefinden. Schön sein kann aber nur, was gut gemacht ist.
>
> **4. Gutes Design macht ein Produkt verständlich.**
> Es verdeutlicht auf einleuchtende Weise die Struktur des Produkts. Mehr noch: Es kann das Produkt zum Sprechen bringen. Im besten Fall erklärt es sich dann selbst.
>
> **5. Gutes Design ist unaufdringlich.**
> Produkte, die einen Zweck erfüllen, haben Werkzeugcharakter. Sie sind weder dekorative Objekte noch Kunstwerke. Ihr Design sollte deshalb neutral sein, die Geräte zurücktreten lassen und dem Menschen Raum zur Selbstverwirklichung geben.
>
> **6. Gutes Design ist ehrlich.**
> Es lässt ein Produkt nicht innovativer, leistungsfähiger, wertvoller erscheinen, als es in Wirklichkeit ist. Es versucht nicht, den Verbraucher durch Versprechen zu manipulieren, die es dann nicht halten kann.
>
> **7. Gutes Design ist langlebig.**
> Es vermeidet, modisch zu sein, und wirkt deshalb nie antiquiert. Im deutlichen Gegensatz zu kurzlebigem Mode-Design überdauert es auch in der heutigen Wegwerfgesellschaft lange Jahre.
>
> **8. Gutes Design ist konsequent bis ins letzte Detail.**
> Nichts darf der Willkür oder dem Zufall überlassen werden. Gründlichkeit und Genauigkeit der Gestaltung sind letztlich Ausdruck des Respekts dem Verbraucher gegenüber.
>
> **9. Gutes Design ist umweltfreundlich.**
> Design leistet einen wichtigen Beitrag zur Erhaltung der Umwelt. Es bezieht die Schonung der Ressourcen ebenso wie die Minimierung von physischer und visueller Verschmutzung in die Produktgestaltung ein.
>
> **10. Gutes Design ist so wenig Design wie möglich.**
> Weniger Design ist mehr, konzentriert es sich doch auf das Wesentliche, statt die Produkte mit Überflüssigem zu befrachten. Zurück zum Puren, zum Einfachen!
>
> Simplizität ist der Schlüssel zur Exzellenz.
>
> — 来源：rams-foundation.org

### 1.3 Rams 本人的扩展阐释（1993 年 Domus 版）

在 1993 年 *Domus* 杂志中，Rams 为十条原则提供了更长篇幅的阐释。🏛️ 以下是关键增补：

**原则 1（创新）**:
> "Innovation is a catchword nowadays, which is often used to mean spectacular novelties, which can be limited to exterior changes in facade. Innovation thus becomes an end in itself. Most of the products I am and was responsible for, have practically become prototypes because they are in their totality longlasting and from both technically and a design cost-benefit point of view, innovative."

**原则 2（有用）**:
> "Design may not reduce people to machines. For instance, a chair certainly has many other functions within a home than only to be sat upon. ... There are no instant recipes for this. We must endeavour to sift out the relevant aspects by discussion with marketing, development and production teams."

**原则 5（不唐突）**:
> "The central question is: is design art, applied art, or is it technology? With an eye to the future, there is only one answer in my opinion: industrial design is technology. ... Everything else is only cosmetic."

**原则 6（诚实）**:
> "In my opinion, designers have an educational task because he or she participates in the social and cultural development and cannot deny this responsibility."

**原则 10（尽可能少的设计）**:
> "Our only real chance is to return to simplicity. ... The biggest design deficit is in my opinion in the basics. ... questioning whether a new product is really necessary. Is the old product which has proved itself still sufficient or is an improvement reasonable?"

以及 Rams 的总结性宣言：
> "Design indeed is pre-eminently a thinking process."

### 1.4 Rams 后来的反思与修正

Rams 始终拒绝将十诫教条化。在多个场合他强调：

> "These points cannot be set in stone because just as technology and culture are constantly developing, so are ideas about good design." 🏛️
> — Metropolis 杂志采访, 2004

在 2009 年东京宣言（Tokyo Manifesto）中，他进一步指出单靠十条原则还不够：

> "Very good design, however, does not evolve only by ticking those ten boxes. From a good, ordinary design there should always be the possibility for an outstanding, self-explanatory design to arise. It very seldom happens. Such outstanding products and their aura of being close to perfection are an essential stimulus to encourage how we design our environment. They are the benchmark for the future." 🏛️

在 2022 年 90 岁生日声明中，Rams 更新了他的表述：

> "We must engage in fewer things, and specifically in ones that are more useful, more environmentally friendly, more universal, and more fascinating. ... Attractivity must be related to new, convincing concepts and functions, not to the pretty appearance designed as a superfluous incentive to buy." 🏛️

### 🎯 UI 设计判断可应用性

| 原则 | UI/UX 映射 |
|---|---|
| 1. 创新 | 真正的交互创新必须伴随技术进步（如 LLM 驱动的 NLUI），而非仅视觉改版 |
| 2. 有用 | 功能优先级 > 视觉装饰；满足心理需求（信任感、安全感）也是"有用" |
| 3. 美学 | "只有做得好才美"：像素级精准、动效流畅 = 审美完整性 |
| 4. 可理解 | "自解释"：零学习曲线的交互是最优的（对标 Don Norman 的 affordance） |
| 5. 不唐突 | 工具性格：UI 应"像好管家"——需时在场，不需时退后 |
| 6. 诚实 | 不操纵用户：Dark patterns 直接违背。承诺不可兑现 = 设计不诚实 |
| 7. 持久 | 不追逐设计趋势（如盲目跟风 glassmorphism / neumorphism） |
| 8. 细节一致 | 每一个 spacing、每一个 hover state 都经过深思 |
| 9. 环境友好 | 数字维度：代码效率、能耗（暗色模式）、数据节俭 |
| 10. 尽可能少的设计 | 删除直到只剩本质：每一个 UI 元素都要为其存在辩护 |

## 2. 《Less but Better / Weniger, aber besser》

### 2.1 基本信息

- **书名**：*Less but Better / Weniger, aber besser*（德英双语）
- **作者**：Dieter Rams
- **编者/出版人**：Jo Klatt（Design+Design 杂志前出版人，Braun 产品收藏家）
- **出版社**：gestalten
- **页数**：154 页
- **初版**：1995 年
- **修订版**：gestalten 新版 📖

### 2.2 核心论点

gestalten 官方介绍概括了该书的定位 📖：

> "Less but Better does not set out to be a complete documentation of Dieter Rams's body of work, nor does it claim to tell the full story of the company Braun. Rather the book explores the ideas, criteria, and methods behind Rams's creations and reveals how a shifting culture of product manufacturing gave rise to universal design benchmarks. From his reflections on design ethics and values we can distill a clear paradigm for future design—because one principle has remained firm over the years: less is simply better."

核心论点提炼：
1. **设计首先是伦理问题**，其次才是美学问题——形式是价值观的表达。
2. **"少"不是目的，"更好"才是**——减少的意义在于聚焦本质。
3. **设计语言必须在公司内部生长**，外包设计无法实现系统性品质。
4. **制造文化决定设计基准**——Braun 的工程驱动 + 设计主导 = 品质保障。
5. **好设计反对"用完即弃"社会**——耐久性本身就是可持续性。

### 2.3 关键章节

基于多个二手来源摘要 [待核实确切章节标题] 📖：

- **设计十诫的完整阐释**：每条原则配有 Rams 的评论与案例
- **Braun 的设计方法论**：工程与设计如何协作
- **手工艺根源**：Rams 祖父的木匠传统对他的影响——"事物的简约直接"
- **视觉污染（Visual Pollution）概念**：形式、色彩、噪音的"不可穿透的混乱"
- **"消费者"vs"使用者"**：Rams 区分 *Verbraucher*（消耗者）和 *Gebraucher*（使用者），主张为后者设计

### 🎯 UI 设计判断可应用性

- "设计首先是伦理问题"：产品策略决策中，商业 KPI 不能凌驾于用户福祉之上
- "制造文化决定设计基准" ⇄ "工程文化决定产品品质"：Design System 不是锦上添花，是工程基础设施
- "Verbraucher vs. Gebraucher"：数字产品应培养"使用者"而非"成瘾者"

## 4. 设计哲学文章与演讲

### 4.1 "Design by Vitsœ"（1976 年纽约演讲）🏛️

这是 Rams 最重要的非产品哲学宣言，比十诫更早，更"原生态"。核心段落：

**关于人与设计**：
> "You cannot understand good design if you do not understand people; design is made for people. It must be ergonomically correct, meaning it must harmonise with a human being's strengths, dimensions, senses and understanding."

**关于产品系统与家居**：
> "A product must be functional in itself but it also must function as part of a wider system: the home."

**关于 Vitsœ 家具的匿名性**：
> "Vitsœ's furniture does not shout; it performs its function in relative anonymity alongside furniture from any designer and in homes from any era."

**关于使用者 vs 消费者**：
> "We make the effort to produce products like this for the intelligent and responsible users – not consumers – who consciously select products that they can really use."

**关于秩序与可理解性**：
> "All objects that are to be used must be subject to a clear order. The remarkable order of design at Vitsœ has the purpose of communicating the function of the object to the user. The design of a Vitsœ product clearly points out its purpose and its use – and facilitates them."

**关于环境**——早于 Brundtland 委员会 7 年：
> "I imagine our current situation will cause future generations to shudder at the thoughtlessness in the way in which we today fill our homes, our cities and our landscape with a chaos of assorted junk."

**关于比例与和谐**：
> "An often-cited feature of the Vitsœ collection is its balance, its harmony, its belonging together. All structures, components and finishes coexist as a well-balanced and harmonious design that gives it usability."

### 4.2 Tokyo Manifesto（2009）🏛️

2009 年日本大型回顾展之际，Rams 面向年轻设计师发表宣言：

> "I would like to encourage the young designers of tomorrow to be self-confident and audacious and ready to take risks. I would also like to invite them to come up with new proposals – not the obvious and the fashionable, but the truly better ones."

关于"非常优秀的设计"：
> "Very good design ... does not evolve only by ticking those ten boxes. ... Such outstanding products and their aura of being close to perfection ... are the benchmark for the future."

关于品牌与品质：
> "An excessive hype about brands has distracted from the original, true qualities of products and opened up the market for cheap, fake offers where the true or false label became more important than the product itself."

关于日本传统作为灵感：
> "Engaging with this country's traditions and making use of them as a stimulus for the future – such as the ancient Zen Buddhism or the Wabi-Sabi – could revive our world of products. To follow the thoughts on modesty or frugality for a certain time could be the beginning of an altogether new understanding of products."

关于设计师的新角色：
> "Designers have to worry about an all-embracing quality of their realised ideas. Design therefore begins with consideration."

### 4.3 iF Design Foundation 问候文（2021）🏛️

Rams 为 *Designing Design Education* 白皮书撰写的问候文，是其晚年思想的重要凝练：

> "Design ist in allererster Linie ein Denkprozess."
> （"设计首先且最重要的，是一个思考过程。"）

> "»Weniger, aber besser« bedeutet daher für mich, dass wir weg kommen müssen von der Unkultur des Überflusses, der Verschwendung, der Billigkeit im Wortsinn, aber auch im übertragenden Sinne. Es bedeutet, dass wir mehr Dinge benötigen, die wirklich das sind und das leisten, was die Benutzer von ihnen erhoffen: Erleichterung, Erweiterung, Intensivierung des Lebens."
> （"'少，但更好'因此对我来说意味着，我们必须远离过剩、浪费与廉价的文化——无论是字面意义还是比喻意义上。这意味着我们需要更多真正能实现使用者期望的东西：让生活更轻松、更宽广、更强烈。"）

> "Tatsächlich ist Gleichgültigkeit gegenüber den Menschen und ihrer Lebenswirklichkeit in meinen Augen die einzige Sünde, die ein eine Gestalterin oder ein Gestalter begehen kann."
> （"事实上，对人的冷漠以及对他们生活现实的漠视，在我眼中是设计师唯一可能犯下的罪。"）

对未来设计的展望：
> "Dazu muss ein Studium zum Industriedesign neue Antworten finden, die neue Parameter zu berücksichtigen haben: Enorme ökologische Herausforderungen, interaktive Prozessabläufe, umfängliche Digitalisierungen, neue Kommunikationsformen, Künstliche Intelligenz und vor allem eine neue Praxis des Konsums..."
> （"工业设计的学习必须找到新的答案，纳入新的参数：巨大的生态挑战、交互式流程、广泛的数字化、新的通信形式、人工智能，以及最重要的——新的消费实践……"）

关于学科交叉：
> "Ich persönlich fände einen intensiven akademischen Austausch mit Architekten und Städteplanern dabei äußerst sinnvoll. Die Umweltgestaltung, der umbaute Raum und die Gerätschaften, also das industrielle Design, gehören zusammen..."
> （"我个人认为与建筑师和城市规划师的紧密学术交流极为重要。环境设计、建筑空间与器具——即工业设计——属于一个整体。"）

### 4.4 Vitsœ 品牌宣言

Vitsœ 官网的 "Good Design" 页面汇集了 Rams 哲学作为公司基因的表达 📖：

> "Here at Vitsœ we stand for the inordinate power of good design in everything we do: designing thoughtfully, responsibly and intelligently for our company, our furniture and the many people who share a profound interest in all of our tomorrows."

> "Living better, with less, that lasts longer."

Rams 本人对与 Vitsœ 关系的描述：
> "In 1957 I began to develop a storage system that formed the basis of the company Vitsœ, which was founded in 1959. Thus the ideology behind my design is engrained within the company." 🏛️

### 🎯 UI 设计判断可应用性

- "Design is pre-eminently a thinking process"：写代码之前先想清楚。快速原型 ≠ 快速思考。
- "产品不喊叫"：UI 不应争夺注意力——通知、弹窗、动效都应为用户任务服务，而非为 engagement 指标
- "furniture does not shout; it performs its function in relative anonymity" = 最好的 UI 是 invisible UI
- "Good design must be able to coexist"：数字产品需要互操作性，而非围墙花园
- "all-embracing quality"：涵盖性能、可访问性、隐私、伦理的全面品质观
- 教师-建筑师-城市规划师交叉：UX 设计师需要与工程师、产品经理、数据科学家深度协作

## 5. "Weniger, aber besser"（少，但更好）

### 5.1 起源

该口号的直接前身是 Mies van der Rohe 的 "Weniger ist mehr"（Less is more，少即是多）。Rams 将其改造为 "Weniger, aber besser"（Less, but better）——关键变化是将中性的 "ist"（是）替换为转折连词 "aber"（但），并将 "mehr"（更多）替换为价值判断 "besser"（更好）。

这一转变的含义：**"少"本身不是美德——只有当"少"带来"更好"时才有价值。**

### 5.2 Rams 对"更少"和"更好"的定义

**更少（Weniger）**：
- 去除一切对核心功能无贡献的元素
- 拒绝"为差异化而差异化"的新造型（批评 Gillette 时代的 Braun）
- 视觉降噪：产品不应在空间中"喊叫"
- 系统的精简而非功能的缺失

**更好（Besser）**：
- 更长的使用周期——不仅是物理耐久，也是审美耐久（不会"视觉上变得难以忍受"）
- 更高的制造品质：只有做好才美（"Schön sein kann aber nur, was gut gemacht ist"）
- 更深的用户理解：满足心理和审美需求，而非仅功能需求
- 更负责任：对环境和未来世代

2017 年 Kinfolk 采访中 Rams 说：

> "I have always tended to steer well clear from this discussion about beauty and argued instead for a design that is as reduced, clear and user-oriented as possible, and simply more bearable for a longer period of time. But 'simple' is especially hard to achieve; even Leonardo da Vinci knew that." 🏛️

> "The only plausible way forward is the less-but-better way: back to purity, back to simplicity. Simplicity is the key to excellence!" 🏛️

### 5.3 口号的演变

从 1970 年代至今，Rams 对 "Weniger, aber besser" 的阐述不断加深：

| 年代 | 侧重点 |
|---|---|
| 1970s | 审美简化：反对形式、色彩、噪音的混乱 |
| 1976 | 伦理维度：资源有限，"无思想消费的时代已经结束" |
| 1993 | 系统思维："设计首先是思考过程" |
| 2009 | 全球化视角：引用日本 Wabi-Sabi，呼吁跨文化复兴 |
| 2021-2023 | 紧迫感：气候危机 + "我们还在消费更多而非更少" |

在最新采访中他强调 "weniger, aber besser" 现在意味着对抗整个消费主义系统：

> "Of course, ethically justifiable design should also be an issue for managers, because it is not the designer alone who decides about what is produced and what not. ... Design should really be a mandatory course for anyone studying business." 🏛️

### 🎯 UI 设计判断可应用性

**关键测试问题**（衍生自 Rams 的 15 个设计团队自查问题）：

1. 这个功能真的必要吗？还是已有成熟方案？
2. 它是丰富用户的生活，还是仅仅迎合他们的占有欲/攀比心/地位焦虑？
3. 它是为短期还是长期而设计？它会加速"丢弃循环"还是减缓它？
4. 它使人更自由还是更依赖？
5. 它替代了什么人类活动？那真的能被称为进步吗？
6. 用户能自己修改变它吗？（= 软件的灵活性/可配置性）
7. 它鼓励好奇心还是被动性？

## 6. Rams 对数字设计的看法

### 6.1 直接言论

Rams 不使用互联网或社交媒体。在 2023 年 Capsule 采访中：

> "I don't use either. It's all very suspicious to me, and I don't want to sit in front of the computer for hours. I prefer tending to my bonsai garden and thinking on my own. This includes close reading of books and newspapers. The old media still functions quite well in my opinion — and, in some ways, even better." 🏛️

关于数字工具对设计过程的影响：

> "Nevertheless, digitality is certainly quite important nowadays. Models can be produced much more quickly than they used to be, although speedy renderings also result in designs that have not been thought completely through because everything looks so good at first glance. Designers should always think first about whether their designs really represent progress. Design begins in our heads, not in computers. Bringing our first ideas to paper by hand is, in my opinion, still a valid process." 🏛️

### 6.2 对 Apple 的评价

Rams 对 Apple 持复杂态度：

**美学层面——高度赞赏** 🏛️：
> "In terms of aesthetics, I think their designs are brilliant. I don't consider it an imitation. I take it as a compliment."

> "It would look like one of Apple's products."（如果让他设计一台电脑）

**伦理层面——存在批判** 🌐：
> "Apple has achieved something I never did" — 指 Apple 将好设计变成了大众市场的成功。

但策展人 Colin Fanning 指出根本差异 📖：
> "That philosophy of durability and longevity is totally missing from the Apple equation, where it's about planned obsolescence and bringing out new models year after year."

Rams 在 Gary Hustwit 纪录片中也表达了这种张力。Hustwit 说 🌐：
> "Every time he crosses a street and sees people glued to their phones, he just can't conceive of how we got to this point. I think he's a little bit saddened by all this work they did in the fifties and sixties, which had a very idealistic point of view, being a stepping stone to where we are now, and the current lust for technology."

### 6.3 对创新的定义——关键区分

Rams 区分了真正的创新和表面的"创新" 🏛️：

> "I've always been of the opinion that new technologies—for example, when I said in the first of my 10 principles that innovation is an important aspect, I meant that technical innovation will get us further, not innovation in terms of appearances. Innovation has to come from the inside and then influence the outside. That's what I understand as innovation."

这对数字设计有直接映射：**真正的 UI 创新源于底层交互模型的变革（如触屏、语音、AI agent），而非视觉层改版。**

### 6.4 如果重新选择

> "If I had something to do in this world again, I would not want to be a designer. Because I believe, in the future, it will be less important to have many things and more important to exercise care about where and how we live." 🏛️

这不是对设计的否定，而是对设计范畴的扩展——从"造物"到"关怀我们如何生活"。

### 🎯 UI 设计判断可应用性

| Rams 的立场 | UI/UX 映射 |
|---|---|
| 不使用社交媒体 | 设计师应警惕自己的"产品"是否在制造成瘾 |
| "Design begins in our heads, not in computers" | Figma 不是设计的起点。"空白纸+思考"先于"画布+组件" |
| "Speedy renderings make things look good at first glance" | 高保真原型可能掩盖交互逻辑缺陷——先在低保真上验证 |
| "Innovation has to come from the inside" | 视觉改版 ≠ 创新。AI-native 交互模式才是真正的创新 |
| 对人们被手机支配感到悲伤 | 你的产品是在解放用户还是奴役用户？这是根本伦理问题 |
| Apple 美学好但"计划报废"违背第 7 条 | 漂亮 ≠ 好。长期价值 > 发布周期 |
| 宁可做景观建筑师 | 设计的不只是界面，是用户的生活环境（Digital Environment） |

## 附录：Rams 的 15 个设计团队自查问题（完整版）

来源：Sophie Lovell, *Dieter Rams: As Little Design as Possible*（转引自 BYU Design Review）📖

> 1. The first question is not if one should be designing something but how.
> 2. Is the product that we are designing really necessary? Are there not already other, similar tried and tested appliances that people have got used to and are good and functional? Is innovation in this instance really necessary?
> 3. Will it really enrich people's lives or does it just appeal to their covetousness, possessiveness or ideas of status? Or does it wake desire because it is offering something new?
> 4. Is it conceived for the short- or long-term, does it just help increase the speed of the cycle of throwaway goods or does it help to slow it down?
> 5. Can it be simply repaired or does it rely on an expensive customer service facility? Can it in fact be repaired at all or is the whole appliance rendered redundant when just one part of it breaks?
> 6. Does it exhibit fashionable, and therefore aesthetically short-lived, design elements?
> 7. Does it help people or incapacitate them? Does it make them more free or more dependent?
> 8. Is it so accomplished and perfect that it perhaps incapacitates or humiliates you?
> 9. Which previous human activity does it replace and can that really be called progress?
> 10. What possibilities for change, what scope does the product offer people?
> 11. Can the product be used in other, perhaps playful, ways?
> 12. Does the product really offer convenience or does it encourage passivity?
> 13. What does the expected improvement look like in the broader context?
> 14. Does it make an action or activity on the whole more complicated or simpler; is it easy to operate or do you have to learn how to use it?
> 15. Does it arouse curiosity and the imagination? Does it encourage desire to use it, understand it and even to change it?
