## 1. 纪录片《Rams》（Gary Hustwit, 2018）

**基本信息：** 74分钟，导演 Gary Hustwit（此前以设计三部曲《Helvetica》《Objectified》《Urbanized》闻名），配乐 Brian Eno。2018年末上映。

### 1.1 拍摄背景：说服 Rams 最后一谈

Rams 以极度抗拒媒体著称。Hustwit 花了数月说服他参与：

> "Dieter doesn't like being interviewed; he doesn't like having strangers in his house moving objects and disrupting his world. What's more, he thinks there are enough books that discuss his work and his convictions."
> — Gary Hustwit, Stylepark 访谈 (2018.11.13)

Vitsœ 的 CEO Mark Adams 贡献了关键说服逻辑：Rams 担心自己 65 年的专业经验会随他而去——"If we have one unique characteristic as human beings that sets us apart from animals, it's the ability to transmit our knowledge." Hustwit 用这个论点打动了他：**这部电影是他向新一代传递思想的最后机会**。

拍摄历时三年（2015–2018），Hustwit 在 Kronberg 的 Rams 家中、旅行途中、展览现场跟拍。据 Hustwit 透露，Rams 明言"这是最后一次接受访问"，他厌倦了重复同样的话。

### 1.2 核心对白与 Rams 的直接引语

**关于"少就是好"的升级声明：**

> "Less would be better everywhere."
> — Dieter Rams，《Rams》(2018)

这一变体将他经典的 "Less, but better" 从产品设计原则扩展为**生活方式和社会行为**的普遍批评。

**关于数字化侵蚀感官体验：**

> "I am of the opinion that all this digitization now is becoming more and more a part of our life. I think it diminishes our ability to experience things. There are pictures that disappear, one after the other, without leaving traces up here (pointing to his head). This goes insanely fast. And maybe that's why we can, or we want to, consume so much. The world that can be perceived through the senses exudes an aura that I believe cannot be digitized. We have to be careful now, that we rule over the digital world, and are not ruled by it."
> — Dieter Rams，引自 Cult of Mac / 影片独白 (2018)

**关于冗余物的未来：**

> "We have to get away from the 'un-culture' of abundance. Because there is no future with so many redundant things. 'Less, but better' is not just a design concept. It's also about our behavior."
> — Dieter Rams，引自 Metropolis Magazine (2018.11)

**Rams 对设计界的最后挑战：**

> "In a way, he's kind of challenging the design world in this film, sort of laying down a challenge to do better."
> "San Francisco is the center of the design world packed with all these people, and they're listening to this 86-year-old German guy in his backyard for an hour and a half, about like, how they're f---ing up. And they're loving it, and they're laughing."
> — Gary Hustwit, Fast Company (2018.10.08)

### 1.3 影片风格与 Rams 哲学的同构

Hustwit 刻意将影片形式与内容统一：

> "The goal was to keep it as simple as possible. It just wouldn't be appropriate to make a cluttered film about Dieter Rams. Less but better, as Dieter would say."
> "We had a copy of the principles tacked to the wall of the editing room during the editing process. It was something we kept coming back to."
> — Gary Hustwit, Dezeen (2019.03.05)

影片只有少数受访者（Mark Adams、Naoto Fukasawa、Sophie Lovell、Mateo Kries、Dietrich Lubs），简化剪辑，配合 Brian Eno 的环境音乐，形成视觉和听觉的双重克制。

### 1.4 Rams 的悔恨主题

影片的核心情绪并非庆祝，而是**深刻的矛盾与悔恨**：

> "Dieter recognises that he did play an instrumental part in driving the culture of overconsumption that we're currently in, from his time at Braun."
> "He says himself that if he could have done it all over again, he would not have chosen to become a designer. He really wanted to be a landscape architect or an urban planner."
> — Gary Hustwit, Dezeen (2019.03.05)

> "He feels a responsibility, that his work had a role in getting to where we are now."
> — Gary Hustwit, AIGA Eye on Design (2018.10.03)

## 2. 深度访谈精选

### 2.1 Hans Ulrich Obrist × Dieter Rams（Capsule Magazine, 2023.04）
**来源：** [rams-foundation.org](https://rams-foundation.org/magazine/interviews/capsule/)

这是近年来最全面、最坦诚的 Rams 访谈之一。由知名策展人 Hans Ulrich Obrist 主持。

**关于"十原则"的起源：**

Rams 澄清原则最初是 Braun 内部项目，从六条扩展为十条，环境考量从一开始就重要："Since around 1970, anyone who could read has been familiar with the warnings of the Club of Rome." 他强调这些不是"manifesto"（宣言），而是"intentionally chosen principles that should be considered and discussed"。

**关于商业教育的失败：**

> "While business students learn a lot about marketing, they are not taught the tenets of good design. That means that in addition to profit and growth, corporate goals should focus on the meaningfulness of their products. Design should really be a mandatory course for anyone studying business."

**关于德国战后设计的伦理：**

> "My experience of the destruction and chaos of this gruesome war as a child and teenager turned me into an avowed pacifist, which I still am to this day."

**关于 90 岁生日的声明（2022）：**

> "We must engage in fewer things, and specifically in ones that are more useful, more environmentally friendly, more universal, and more fascinating. In my opinion, that is the key to transforming mindless consumerism to responsible consumerism. Attractivity must be related to new, convincing concepts and functions, not to the pretty appearance designed as a superfluous incentive to buy."

**关于数字文化与设计：**

> "I don't use either [internet or social media]. It's all very suspicious to me, and I don't want to sit in front of the computer for hours. I prefer tending to my bonsai garden and thinking on my own. This includes close reading of books and newspapers. The old media still functions quite well in my opinion."
> "Design begins in our heads, not in computers. Bringing our first ideas to paper by hand is, in my opinion, still a valid process."

**对设计界"沙盒游戏"的批评：**

> "That a once-serious design journal such as form is now publishing antiprinciples entitled 'Grilling Rams' and supporting dysfunctional, resistant design is scandalous and does little to further the discussion about the future."

### 2.2 Gary Hustwit × Dieter Rams（Fast Company / Objectified Interview, 2015.03）
**来源：** [fastcompany.com](https://www.fastcompany.com/3043815/)

这是 Hustwit 在《Objectified》时期进行的长篇访谈，后成为纪录片项目的前奏。

**建筑学对工业设计的影响：**

Rams 详细解释了他的建筑训练——从 Wiesbaden 工艺学校到 Skidmore, Owings & Merrill 的合作——如何塑造了他对细节和前期规划的态度：

> "In industrial design, everything for the production has to be clarified in advance with models and prototypes, all the details, for multiple parts. Otherwise you don't proceed to the production stage. You have to think carefully in advance about what you're making and how you will make it, because for both architecture and industrial design, the cost of changing things afterward is much higher than the cost of better preparation."

**对糟糕设计的愤怒：**

> "What's your reaction when you see objects that have been poorly designed?"
> "It angers me. What winds me up most is the modern kitsch. Older things make me less angry; they are the past. But I'm angry at the many bad, false things of which there are still too many of in this world. Unnecessary, false, dishonest products; these are the things that make me angry."

**首次说出"不想再做设计师"：**

> "If I had something to do in this world again, I would not want to be a designer. Because I believe, in the future, it will be less important to have many things and more important to exercise care about where and how we live."

### 2.3 Kinfolk / Dezeen 访谈（2017.02）
**来源：** [dezeen.com](https://www.dezeen.com/2017/02/24/dieter-rams-designer-interview-simplicity-key-excellence/)

Rams 此时 84 岁，接受了一次罕见的深度文字访谈，发表于 Kinfolk 杂志。

**核心声明：**

> "Simplicity is the key to excellence!"

> "I have always tended to steer well clear from this discussion about beauty and argued instead for a design that is as reduced, clear and user-oriented as possible, and simply more bearable for a longer period of time. But 'simple' is especially hard to achieve; even Leonardo da Vinci knew that."

**压抑美学以换取耐久：**

> "For me, a restrained aesthetic and function that is as optimised as possible have always been important. These qualities lead to long utilisation cycles: The objects do not become visually unbearable after a short time because they have not pushed themselves into the foreground. Certainly, these qualities also act as a constraint upon innovation. We really should consider very carefully whether we constantly need new things."

**"舒适"而非"奢侈"：**

> "I completely concur with Adolf Behne that we need 'comfort' instead of 'luxury.' He believed that really good design should not be fuel for consumption that brings us nothing but irreparable resource problems and environmental destruction."

### 2.4 Alessandro Mendini × Dieter Rams（Domus, 1984.04）
**来源：** [domusweb.it](https://www.domusweb.it/en/from-the-archive/2012/05/19/alessandro-mendini-interviews-dieter-rams.html)

1984 年的珍贵档案：意大利后现代主义旗手 Mendini 与德国功能主义大师的跨风格对话。

**Rams 对功能主义的非教条态度：**

> "I am against all 'isms', including 'functionalism'."

> "The important principle in design in my mind is to remove the non-essential in order to bring out the essential. Good design means to me the least possible design."

**对"情感刺激型设计"的警告——在 1984 年就已预见体验设计的陷阱：**

> "I have nothing against a stimulating and fascinating design, but I would like to declare my opposition to a design which is chiefly concerned with stimuli. Our culture is our abode. And I mean our everyday culture, with the products for which we designers are responsible. A design intended at all costs to be emotive, original, aggressive and striking leads to alienation and stress."

**自称"形式工程师"（Gestalt-Ingenieur）：**

> "I suppose I really see myself more as an engineer of form than as an artist or poet."

**对未来的预言：**

> "Mass production will have to transform itself into quality production."

### 2.5 Designboom 视频访谈（2000）
**来源：** [designboom.com](https://www.designboom.com/design/dieter-rams-video-interview-treasures-on-tape-01-29-2020/)

2000 年 designboom 在 Rams 家中拍摄的未公开视频片段，2020 年作为 20 周年档案释出。

> "I try to develop devices for everyday use that work well, that don't hurt the eyes (or other senses); and I try to make sure they are made (and sold accordingly) at an acceptable price that ordinary consumers can afford. That is almost all."

> "The power of the designers lies in constant demands, optimism and patience. It is expressed in the visualized example and can thus make a major contribution to clarifying and solving the problems."

> "Indifference to people and their real life is the only sin in my eyes that a designer can commit."

### 2.6 Vitra Design Museum 访谈（2021）
**来源：** [design-museum.de](https://www.design-museum.de/en/ueber-design/interviews/detailseiten/interview-dieter-rams.html)

为展览"German Design 1949–1989"而进行，由 Mateo Kries 主持。

**战后设计的起点的道德含义：**

> "Our first task, really, was to clean up, just like everyone else was doing at the time. And from this necessity came a focus on manual work and workmanship. … Today, we're in a similar position: We need to clean up, to return to the basics."

**对东德设计教育的手工传统的惊讶与尊重：**

Rams 称赞了 Burg Giebichenstein（哈勒）在设计教学中对动手能力的重视，并直言西德的设计教育缺失了"making"这个环节——"which is why so many products have come about that cast doubt on the term 'design'."

**对"design"一词被稀释的批评：**

> "We are constantly getting caught up on the term 'design', this frayed concept, when really we should be talking about a shaping of the environment."
> "Not on us needing yet another new mug, a new radio, or whatever else."

## 3. 展览与讲座

### 3.1 "Less and More: The Design Ethos of Dieter Rams"（2008–2012 世界巡展）
**联合主办：** Museum Angewandte Kunst（法兰克福）+ Suntory Museum（大阪）
**巡展城市：** 大阪、东京、首尔、伦敦（Design Museum, 2009）、旧金山
**观众总数：** 86 周展览期内超过 40 万人次

伦敦站（Design Museum, 2009.11）是 Rams 在英国首次全面回顾展，展出 Brauns 电子产品 + Vitsœ 家具 + 草图 + 模型 + 影片，包括 Jony Ive、Jasper Morrison、Sam Hecht、Naoto Fukasawa 的访谈影像。

**Wallpaper 评价：**
> "With the same reductive efficiency he applied to his own designs, in ten simple commandments, Rams succinctly defined what design for a post-war modern world should be."

### 3.2 "Modular World"（Vitra Design Museum, 2016.11–2017.03）
**策展人：** Heng Zhi（志恒）
**地点：** Vitra Schaudepot（Herzog & de Meuron 设计）
**特色：** 首次以 Rams 家具为主要焦点的展览

Rams 本人对此展览的理念陈述：

> "Perhaps even more directly than with the Braun appliances, my furniture arose from a belief in how the world should be 'outfitted' and how people should live in this artificial environment. In this respect, each piece of furniture is also a design for a certain kind of world and way of living."

**策展人评述：**
> "What is absolutely impressive about Rams' furniture is that it offers real, functioning solutions for sustainable living, and the answer lies in the perfection of design, not recycling."
> — Heng Zhi, Dezeen (2017.02.06)

### 3.3 "Design by Vitsœ" 演讲（1976.12, 纽约）
**来源：** [vitsoe.com](https://www.vitsoe.com/files/assets/1000/17/VITSOE_Dieter_Rams_speech.pdf)

在 Jack Lenor Larsen 的纽约展厅发表。这是 Rams 最具有预言性的公开声明——**比联合国布伦特兰委员会早了七年**：

> "The introduction of good design is of social importance as it means, amongst other things, absolutely avoiding waste."
> "Product design is the total configuration of a product: its form, colour, material and construction. The product should serve its intended purpose efficiently."
> "A designer who wants to regard himself as an artist … is merely dressing-up products in a last minute garment."

他说到"increasing and irreversible shortage of natural resources"，呼吁设计师为产品的全部生命周期负责。2026 年的评论者 Eric Auzenne 如此评价：

> "He'd spent three decades designing things to last, fighting planned obsolescence, arguing that honesty in design was non-negotiable. The market responded by building planned obsolescence into the product specs … He was calling for an 'end to the era of wastefulness' in 1976. The throwaway society he warned about has only gotten more intense."

### 3.4 "Principled Design"（Philadelphia Museum of Art, 2018.11–2019.04）
**策展人：** Colin Fanning
**核心策展思路：** 将 Braun T3 收音机与 Apple iPod 并置——

> "While you can see the visual similarities — they're right there in front of you — I'm inviting folks to think about the underlying ethos."
> "That philosophy of durability and longevity is totally missing from the Apple equation, where it's about planned obsolescence and bringing out new models year after year."

### 3.5 "A Look Back and Ahead"（2021– 巡展）
由 Dieter and Ingeborg Rams Foundation 组织，已巡展至法兰克福、纽约 Goethe-Institut、华盛顿、米兰 ADI Design Museum、威斯巴登 Stadtmuseum。

## 4. Rams 与 Apple / Jony Ive

### 4.1 Rams 对 Apple 的肯定与微妙批评

**正面肯定（The Telegraph, 2011.06）：**

> "I have always regarded Apple products – and the kind words Jony Ive has said about me and my work – as a compliment. Without doubt there are few companies in the world that genuinely understand and practise the power of good design in their products and their businesses."

> "Apple has managed to achieve what I never achieved: using the power of their products to persuade people to queue to buy them. For me, I had to queue to receive food at the end of World War II. That's quite a change."

**关于"设计"一词贬值——Apple 和 Vitsœ 是两个例外：**

> "I am troubled by the devaluing of the word 'design'. I find myself now being somewhat embarrassed to be called a designer. In fact I prefer the German term, Gestalt-Ingenieur. Apple and Vitsoe are relatively lone voices treating the discipline of design seriously in all corners of their businesses."

**关于设计师与 CEO 的关系：**

> "I have always observed that good design can normally only emerge if there is a strong relationship between an entrepreneur and the head of design. At Apple this situation exists – between Steve Jobs and Jony Ive. This was the case at Braun where I always reported to Erwin and Artur Braun."

**当 Starck 指责 Apple 抄袭 Rams 时，Rams 的回应：**

> "I don't feel so, and for me it's a compliment. It's a compliment that they use the basic thinking about what design can be, and how to use new technologies."
> — 引自 SlashGear / Co.Design (2011)

**2015 年，当被问到如果要设计一台电脑会是什么样子：**

> "It would look like one of Apple's products. … In terms of aesthetics, I think their designs are brilliant. I don't consider it an imitation. I take it as a compliment."
> — 引自 Cult of Mac (2015.03.18)

### 4.2 Jony Ive 论 Rams（Rams Foundation, 2024.10）
**来源：** [rams-foundation.org](https://rams-foundation.org/magazine/interviews/jonathan-ive/)

这是 Ive 近年罕见的深度访谈，坦诚回顾了自己与 Rams 的精神渊源：

> "Dieter Rams' philosophy and principles translated into actual products that so resonated with me when I was studying design. That is what I and so many designers owe Dieter. He articulated the way it should be."
> "Dieter Rams in so many ways articulated the framework for the profession that was inspirational and responsible. He described the way as designers that we could work with care and dedication, but also in a responsible way and use our resources and the Earth's resources carefully and thoughtfully."

**Ive 讨论了 Rams 与 Steve Jobs 之间的相似性：**

> "It is that collaboration with the founder/CEO that is critically important. … I do not know any other way than working directly and very closely with the leader of the entire organization."
> "Interestingly, we would talk more about philosophy and approach than specific products."
> "The work that [Rams] did — in terms of building an expert team of industrial designers and architects and graphic designers and the way that they practiced within the company of Braun — that process is often not seen."

**对 Rams 作品体量的敬畏：**

> "To make one thing well is an achievement. To design 400 is extraordinary. To me, his body of work speaks to such a clear sense of how it should be. … It's the fact that Dieter Rams was able to so consistently manifest his philosophy and his values across a broad range of products. To me, that's breathtaking."

### 4.3 隐含的张力：美学继承 vs. 伦理断裂

尽管 Ive 和 Rams 相互尊重，但 Colin Fanning（费城美术馆策展人）犀利地指出了根本矛盾：

> "That philosophy of durability and longevity is totally missing from the Apple equation, where it's about planned obsolescence and bringing out new models year after year."

Hustwit 也观察到 Rams 的隐忧：

> "Every time he crosses a street and sees people glued to their phones, he just can't conceive of how we got to this point. I think he's a little bit saddened by all this work they did in the fifties and sixties, which had a very idealistic point of view, being a stepping stone to where we are now, and the current lust for technology."
> — 1stDibs Introspective (2019.01.11)

**关键悖论：** Apple 继承了 Rams 的形式语言（极简、白色、几何、直觉交互），但背弃了他的伦理核心（耐久、可修、环境友好）。Rams 设计的产品可以用 50 年；iPhone 的软件支持周期不到 10 年。

## 5. 对当代设计的批评

### 5.1 "用完即弃"文化和计划性报废

Rams 在 1976 年就已呼吁终结"浪费的时代"。40 多年后，情况更加恶化：

> "I think he's horrified at [the shift from designing things to last a lifetime to a philosophy where] 'if something breaks, if a household appliance breaks or even the button falls off or something it's like, I can get a new one for 20 bucks.' … That's something I think Dieter is horrified at, but also feels, partly, he had a role in."
> — Gary Hustwit, Salon (2018.12.16)

**在 Apple Store 里的震撼场景：**

> "In one scene, Rams is in an Apple store looking at an iPhone and 'lamenting' how people don't look at each other."
> — Cult of Mac (2018.10.08)

### 5.2 对消费电子的批评

Rams 的核心批评不是针对"技术"本身，而是针对**缺乏思考的消费主义驱动型设计**：

> "A long product cycle is in my opinion an effective means of limiting pointless, rapid overconsumption. Not only should the technology of the products be long-lasting and repairable, the appearance should also be enduring."
> — Rams Foundation / Capsule (2023)

> "The stuff they were doing in the nineteen fifties still looks like the future. … even though much of the technology those products contained is now obsolete."
> — Gary Hustwit, 1stDibs

### 5.3 对数字产品与社交媒体的批评

Rams 本人不使用电脑、不上网、不用社交媒体。这不是卢德主义（Luddism），而是**原则性的抵制**：

> "He's all about simplifying. He spent his whole career thinking about how to simplify our lives with these objects and also simplify his life. So, while digital technology has an amazing amount of capabilities … has it simplified our lives? That's, I think, the question."
> — Gary Hustwit, Salon (2018)

> "I don't use either. It's all very suspicious to me, and I don't want to sit in front of the computer for hours. I prefer tending to my bonsai garden and thinking on my own."
> — Rams Foundation / Capsule (2023)

Hustwit 进一步解读：

> "I think it's one of the reasons Dieter does not have a computer and doesn't do social media and doesn't have email. He's all about simplifying. … So, while digital technology has an amazing amount of capabilities … has it simplified our lives?"

### 5.4 对营销驱动型设计的批评（自 1984 年至今，一以贯之）

在 Braun 被 Gillette 收购后，Rams 与美式营销的冲突日益尖锐：

> "In the 1990s, the American marketing firm of Gillette's parent company won out at Braun and began constantly pushing for new designs."
> "I always strove to use the Braun logo in the most unobtrusive way possible. … A small logo is much more powerful than a large one."
> "Of course, this led to many a heated discussion with the marketing department."
> — Rams Foundation / Capsule (2023)

他早在 1984 年就对 Mendini 说：

> "I feel ill at ease, two trends in particular seem to me dangerous: the speed with which experiments are passed off as finished solutions and presented as such to the public; and the massive way in which expressive media are harnessed to arouse emotive stimuli."

### 5.5 对快时尚？——隐含批评

虽然 Rams 几乎不直接谈论服装，但其核心原则——耐久性、诚实、环保——与快时尚完全对立。他的批评框架可以无缝应用于任何"快速生产-快速消费-快速废弃"的行业。

### 5.6 对设计教育界"玩世不恭"的愤怒

> "There are articles in the papers I read in which design is viewed as a sandbox game. That a once-serious design journal such as form is now publishing antiprinciples entitled 'Grilling Rams' and supporting dysfunctional, resistant design is scandalous."
> — Rams Foundation / Capsule (2023)

## 6. 退休后反思与遗产

### 6.1 "如果重新来过，我不想再做设计师"

这是 Rams 晚年最具冲击力的声明——出现在多个场合、多种语境中，贯穿 2015 年到 2023 年：

> "If I had something to do in this world again, I would not want to be a designer."
> — Fast Company (2015)

> "I would be a landscape architect or urban planner. The world's problems are so large today that it is no longer only about the realms of objects for personal use."
> — Rams Foundation / Capsule (2023)

> "Man muss nicht beim Topf oder bei der Gabel anfangen, man muss bei der Landschaft anfangen."（你不必从锅碗瓢盆开始，你必须从景观开始。）
> — SZ Magazin (2012.11)

在 WHYY 采访（2018）中，他说：

> "I would have started with landscape planning, not city planning. We have to start with the landscape, then come to cities. We are on the way to disturb our planet. We are making too many mistakes."

### 6.2 Dieter and Ingeborg Rams Foundation（1992 年成立）

2023 年进行了重大重组。Rams 和妻子 Ingeborg 将大部分私人资产转入基金会（Ingeborg 于 2023 年 5 月去世，未能见到新基金会全面运作）。新董事会包括法兰克福建筑师 Till Schneider。Klaus Klemp 担任执行总监。

基金会正在建设一个学术档案，包含 7000+ 照片、文本、讲稿、访谈、通信、影片和草图模型。与法兰克福应用艺术博物馆（Museum Angewandte Kunst, Richard Meier 建筑）合作保存。

基金会的使命：

> "The foundation collaborates with the Museum of Applied Arts in Frankfurt, where you are putting together your personal archive."
> "Together we are going to plan the long-term direction in the coming months and discuss possible new areas of action. My ten principles of design will certainly play a role there."

### 6.3 93 岁的 Rams 在看什么？

虽然 2026 年没有最新的公开访谈记录，但可以从 Rams 的长期关注推断：

- **景观与城市规划**——这是他反复说"如果重来"会选择的领域
- **气候危机**——"The climate catastrophe can no longer be denied, cars are getting larger and larger, and we are consuming more instead of less"
- **地缘政治**——对普京的侵略战争、中美对抗感到忧虑："I am sometimes very skeptical of the future"
- **盆景园艺**——"I prefer tending to my bonsai garden and thinking on my own"
- **书籍和报纸**——"The old media still functions quite well in my opinion"
- **Vitsœ 的持续合作**——606 通用搁架系统仍在全球销售，620 Chair Programme 仍在生产

### 6.4 Rams 的遗产张力

Rams 的遗产包含一个深刻悖论：

1. **形式遗产**：iPhone、iPad、iMac —— 几乎所有当代消费电子产品的视觉语言都源于 Braun 在 1950s–60s 建立的模板
2. **伦理遗产**：十原则、环境责任、反计划性报废、反对过度消费——正被同一个产业系统性地背叛

Hustwit 在 Core77 访谈（2018）中总结了这种矛盾：

> "The contradiction of Dieter regretting being a designer and feeling like he played a part in getting us to the consumerist point we're at is exactly why I wanted to do this."

Colin Fanning 在费城美术馆展览中进一步表达：

> "Despite the depth to which Ramsian rhetoric has penetrated the design profession, today we face a welter of rapidly obsolescing devices, addiction-courting digital interfaces, and the ever-changing terrain of the Internet."

但同时——正因为如此——Rams 的思想比以往任何时候都更有紧迫性。正如 Hustwit 所说：

> "Rams' philosophy is more relevant now than ever."
