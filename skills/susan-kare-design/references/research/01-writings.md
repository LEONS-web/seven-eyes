## 零、引言：为什么叫 "Iconographer"（图标画家）而非 "Graphic Designer"

Kare 并没有明确拒绝 "graphic designer" 这个头衔——她最早的正式职位是 Apple 的 "Macintosh Artist"，后来的公司叫 "Susan Kare Design"。但 MoMA 永久收藏她的作品时，正式称谓是 **"a pioneering and influential computer iconographer"**。[来源: kareprints.com/about]

她自己对此没有做过长篇解释，但从她的自我描述中可以提炼出线索：

- 她的工作对象是**像素级**的：32x32 单色网格上的每一个点都要精确决定
- 她的类比对象是**马赛克、刺绣、针尖工艺**——这些都是某种形式的 "iconography"（图像符号制作），而非传统平面设计
- 她强调自己解决的是 **"how do you make a concept in 16-by-16 black and white dots"** 这种问题——这是符号学+工艺的结合 [来源: Stanford 2000 interview]

> "I still joke that there's nothing new under the sun, and bitmap graphics are like mosaics and needlepoint and other pseudo-digital art forms, all of which I had practiced before going to Apple." [一手, Stanford 2000 interview, https://web.stanford.edu/dept/SUL/sites/mac/primary/interviews/kare/trans.html]

**可信度**: 一手（她本人+MoMA 官方描述）。她从未明确说"我不是 graphic designer"，所以"iconographer"应理解为对她独特技能组合的描述——像素级符号制作+隐喻思维+工艺传统——而非对 graphic design 的否定。

## 一、核心设计哲学（一手言论，按主题组织）

### 1.1 "设计问题靠思考语境和隐喻来解决——不是靠工具"

这是 Kare 在多次访谈中反复强调的最核心观点，几乎成了她的签名句。

> "My philosophy has not really changed — I really try to develop symbols that are meaningful and memorable... design problems are solved by thinking about context and metaphor — not by tools." [一手, Priceonomics interview 2014, 转引自 blog.adafruit.com/2014/04/07/...]

> "Design tools evolve and change, but I believe the fundamental process (evaluating a problem, developing a variety of options, and iterating toward a solution) remains consistent over time." [一手, Cooper Hewitt Q&A 2019, https://www.cooperhewitt.org/2019/09/17/nda-20-yrs-qa-with-susan-kare/]

### 1.2 "好设计的三个标准：Meaningful, Memorable, Clear"

来自 Cooper Hewitt 2019 年国家设计奖 Q&A。当被问及"用三个形容词定义好设计"时，Kare 的回答：

> "Meaningful. Memorable. Clear." [一手, Cooper Hewitt 2019]

这三个词在她的其他访谈中有大量呼应：
- **Meaningful（有意义）**："It has been drummed into me by colleagues and designers I respect that it's of major importance to think about what images mean, not just what they look like." [一手, Cooper Hewitt 2019]
- **Memorable（好记）**："Good icons should be instantly recognizable — even if someone's never seen it, you can ask them what it does, and they get it." [一手, Stanford 2000]
- **Clear（清晰）**："I'm a believer in symbols that are easy to recognize, and limit excessive detail." [一手, TNW 2014]

### 1.3 图标 = 交通标志，而非插画

这可能是 Kare 最广泛被引用的设计比喻。需注意一个微妙的情况：这句话的精确措辞在不同来源中有变化，且其中一版来自她旧个人网站的自我陈述，另一版是记者的转述。

> "I believe that good icons are more akin to road signs rather than illustrations, and ideally should present an idea in a clear, concise, and memorable way." [一手, 来自她旧个人网站 kare.com 的 designer bio（已归档），以及 2001 年 Wendy Marinaccio 采访（Stanford "Making the Macintosh" 项目）]

> "I tend to think of icons more like traffic signs than as illustrations. It's much more successful if it is simple." [据称出自 1996 年 New York Times 采访，但原文链接已无法追溯，转引自多处设计博客——标记为二手/待验证]

**注**: 这两句话（"akin to road signs" vs "more like traffic signs"）的措辞略有不同。前者是她自己在履历中写的（更严谨的措辞），后者可能是记者的转述或简化。本调研以她自述版为准。

### 1.4 "简单 = 普适"——Scott McCloud 的影响

Kare 多次提到 Scott McCloud 的《Understanding Comics》（理解漫画）对她设计思维的关键影响：

> "I remember reading in Scott McCloud's book, Understanding Comics, why more people can 'see' themselves in a simple smile face graphic than a detailed drawing of Prince Valiant. This principle applies to icons: Universality is good. So a very detailed, very specific icon of a certain type of writing implement seems less effective as a symbol." [一手, VentureBeat 2011, https://venturebeat.com/business/macintosh-icons-susan-kare]

> "I also believe that, in general, less detail makes symbols more universal. Scott McCloud (in his terrific book, Understanding Comics) illustrates this by showing a series of faces with less and less detail, until you see a circle with dots for eyes and a line for a mouth — it could represent anyone." [一手, KidsCodecs interview 2020, https://kidscodecs.com/an-interview-with-susan-kare/]

这是一个重要的设计方法论：**抽象程度越高（细节越少），越多人能在其中看到自己**。这不是"简陋"，而是故意留出投射空间。

### 1.5 日常物品作为符号基础——让计算机变得"个人化"

> "I interpreted 'personal' and non-technical to mean that it would be good if symbols were based on everyday objects, when possible. For example, it seemed to me that more people had experience with a wristwatch than an hourglass." [一手, TNW 2014, https://thenextweb.com/news/original-mac-designer-susan-kare-everyday-objects-made-computing-personal]

> "Little pictures and symbols made that computer accessible. So I think in that way design did, and can, serve as a bridge to be a great equalizer." [一手, MoMA 音频导览, https://www.moma.org/audio/playlist/328/363]

**值得注意的对比**: 她选择腕表替代沙漏、选择微笑替代严肃——每一个选择都是在说：普通人的日常经验比精英的学术符号更值得被用作设计语言。

### 1.6 约束是创造力的催化剂

这是 Kare 最被低估但反复出现的主题：

> "What became clear to me was that I really enjoyed the structure of that kind of design challenge, of working with relatively limited screen real estate." [一手, New York Magazine 2016, https://nymag.com/news/features/beginnings/susan-kare/]

> "I've had a lot of practice in thinking deeply about metaphors that make sense and aiming to express those in small grids, especially when constrained by limited screen real estate. Give me 16 x 16 pixels and a concept and I feel fearless." [一手, 多来源转引]

> "Even though I work a lot in vector images now, where pixel count doesn't matter as much, I still feel as though if you have that constraint, I'm your person." [一手, New York Magazine 2016]

> "Constraints are helpful creative production." [一手, Ledger podcast 2025, https://www.ledger.com/blog-susan-kare-nano-gen5-badges]

### 1.7 "太阳底下没有新鲜事"——设计史的连续性

Kare 反复强调数字设计不是凭空创造的，而是人类工艺传统的延续：

> "You study art history and realize there's nothing new under the sun. To think digital art is a new thing no one's done before... have you ever seen 18th-century needlepoint?" [一手, Ledger conversation with Ariel Wengroff 2025]

> "I still joke that there's nothing new under the sun, and bitmap graphics are like mosaics and needlepoint and other pseudo-digital art forms, all of which I had practiced before going to Apple." [一手, Stanford 2000]

> "If you study art history, you learn that there's not much new under the sun, but you can strive to make new connections between images and ideas." [一手, Cooper Hewitt 2019]

这一哲学在两个层面上运作：(1) 谦逊——承认所有设计都有前身；(2) 方法论——你可以利用美术史、民间艺术、广告、瓶盖等一切人类符号史来解决当代设计问题。

### 1.8 "不只是一个解决方案"——避免假设只有一种正确答案

> "When solving a design problem, Susan Kare avoids assuming there is only one solution and usually produces a range of options." [二手, Ledger podcast show notes 2025, 总结她的话]

> "I usually think you end up with something better when there are more ideas." [一手, Ledger podcast 2025]

> "I really try hard to focus on whatever I'm working on — to think about the design problem and a number of possible solutions — but design is not an exact science so there's never one 'right' answer." [一手, KidsCodecs 2020]

### 1.9 迭代与克制

> "I try to think hard about the meaning of icons and look at them in context (in a mockup) and exercise restraint. You don't want the UI to compete with the data." [一手, VentureBeat 2011]

> "It was definitely an iterative process. I always like to work with placeholders and tweak and improve images while there's time." [一手, VentureBeat 2011]

> "I still think this is great — to see graphics in the software they're destined for as early as possible as opposed to 'on paper' or in art files." [一手, TNW 2014]

## 二、重要演讲与访谈（按时间排列）

### 2.1 Stanford 大学口述历史访谈（2000 年 9 月 8 日）

- **采访者**: Alex Pang, "Making the Macintosh" 项目
- **地点**: Kare 家中
- **来源**: https://web.stanford.edu/dept/SUL/sites/mac/primary/interviews/kare/trans.html
- **可信度**: 一手（完整录音文字稿，约 8000 词）

**这是目前已知最详尽、最可靠的一手资料。** 覆盖主题：

- **入职前**: Ph.D. 论文（19-20 世纪雕塑中的漫画手法），受 Andy Hertzfeld 邀请，用 Apple II 交换设计
- **Mac 工作**: 图标编辑器出现之前用十六进制手工输入像素，Andy 做了更好的编辑器后才加速
- **隐喻选择**: 每个图标做多个版本，和团队一起测试。"Copy" 试过镜子、猫在镜子里（copycat）、复印机等——最后选择了更抽象的方案
- **Command 键符号 (⌘)**: 在符号书中找到瑞典露营地标志，"Oh great, it means feature, it's abstract, but it's kind of friendly, and it's really easy to express in pixels." 多年后发现这个符号实际上是鸟瞰城堡图——"It was fun years later to see that it wasn't just abstract."
- **bomb 图标**: "I designed this image as a bomb because I was told they would never be seen by anyone. But unfortunately, that was not the case." 有人看到 bomb 后打电话到 Apple 担心电脑会爆炸。
- **Logo/标题栏**: 花大量时间在标题栏设计上——条纹？建筑细节？——后来发现最好的做法是最简单的
- **国际用户**: 实验过猫在镜子里（"copycat"），放弃，因为双关语不跨文化翻译
- **推荐书籍**: Henry Dreyfuss 的 Symbol Sourcebook；Kanji Pictograms；Paul Rand 著作
- **hobo signs**: "This kind of symbol appeals to me because it had to be really simple, and clear to a group of people who were not going to be studying these for years in academia."

**关键语录**:
> "I always go to books when I'm stuck... I don't think of them as icons, but it shows you that some of the kanji are more iconic than you would think."

> "When you're desperate for an idea — some icons, like the piece of paper, are no problem; but others defy the visual, like 'undo' — you look at things like hobo signs."

> "I'm a huge Paul Rand fan, and I think he always was one of the most articulate, excellent writers and designers. Sometimes when I'm thinking about my own icon work, I read what he wrote about his work, and about trying to put meaning into what you do."

## 三、自创术语与方法论概念

### 3.1 "Economy of Expression"（表达的节约）

这是 Kare 设计哲学的核心操作性概念——在最小的视觉空间中传达最大的意义。

> "I find it really interesting to solve that problem of, how do you make a concept in 16-by-16 black and white dots?" [一手, Stanford 2000]

> "The advantage of working in pixels is that it's iterative. You can consider one approach, try another and see how something looks with one dot off or on." [一手, 多来源转引]

这个概念的操作化含义：
- 每个像素都必须有存在的理由
- 能用 16x16 做出来的绝不用 32x32
- 单色能表达的绝不用多色

### 3.2 "Meaningful and Memorable"（有意义且好记）

这是她最一致的双标准框架，出现在几乎所有访谈中：

> "My philosophy has not really changed — I really try to develop symbols that are meaningful and memorable." [一手, Priceonomics 2014]

> "For designing symbols, I always try to create images that are meaningful and memorable, so they are easy to remember." [一手, KidsCodecs 2020]

后来在 Cooper Hewitt 2019 Q&A 中加入了 "Clear" 形成三词框架。

### 3.3 "Traffic Signs, Not Illustrations"（交通标志，不是插画）

她自述的标准表达是 "good icons are more akin to road signs rather than illustrations"。这个概念包含：
- 即时可识别性
- 跨语言/跨文化通用性
- 简洁到不可再删
- 功能优先于装饰

### 3.4 "The Marriage of Craft and Metaphor"（工艺与隐喻的结合）

> "I loved the puzzle-like nature of working in sixteen-by-sixteen and thirty-two-by-thirty-two pixel icon grids, and the marriage of craft and metaphor." [一手, 多来源转引]

这是她描述自己工作本质的简洁表达——像素工艺（craft）+ 概念隐喻（metaphor）。

### 3.5 "Limited Screen Real Estate"（有限的屏幕地产）

这不是她独创的术语，但她在访谈中反复使用，将其从技术约束转化为设计方法论的核心支柱。

### 3.6 "Design is Not an Exact Science"（设计不是精密科学）

> "I really try hard to... think about the design problem and a number of possible solutions — but design is not an exact science so there's never one 'right' answer." [一手, KidsCodecs 2020]

### 3.7 "Symbols Based on Everyday Objects"（基于日常物品的符号）

这是她让计算机变得"个人化"的核心策略：

> "I interpreted 'personal' and non-technical to mean that it would be good if symbols were based on everyday objects, when possible." [一手, TNW 2014]

### 3.8 "The Continuum"（连续性）

> "I'm not saying that everyone should go look backwards. But I think it's good to remind yourself that most things are a continuum and you can learn from that." [一手, Ledger 2025]

这代表了她的历史观——设计演进是连续的，不是断裂的革命。

## 四、设计流程自述

### 4.1 从草图到像素的完整过程（1982-1984 年）


**阶段 1: 没有工具时的图形纸方法**:
- 购买 "the smallest graph paper" 可以在美术用品店买到
- 手工画出 32x32 的网格（后来 16x16）
- 用彩色笔填满方格——每一格 = 一个屏幕像素
- 这些图形纸草图现在在 MoMA 和 SFMoMA 永久收藏

**阶段 2: 手动十六进制翻译**:
- 在图标编辑器写好之前，Kare 被教如何把每四个像素换算成十六进制值
- 程序员用这些十六进制值把图标渲染到屏幕上

**阶段 3: 图标编辑器**:
- Andy Hertzfeld 编写了图标编辑器，可以同时看到放大的编辑视图和实际大小的预览
- Kare 称之为 "a huge asset"

**阶段 4: MacPaint 直接绘制**:
- "After a while, I started using MacPaint to make the icons." [一手, Stanford 2000]

**阶段 5: 在软件环境中测试**: 
- "I still think this is great — to see graphics in the software they're destined for as early as possible as opposed to 'on paper' or in art files." [一手, TNW 2014]

### 4.2 图标设计的工作流（她自己描述的）

1. **收到概念**: 程序员告诉她需要什么概念的图标（如 "copy", "undo", "save"）
2. **头脑风暴**: "I usually produce a range of options"
3. **查阅参考**: 翻符号书（Dreyfuss Symbol Sourcebook）、kanji 字典、民俗图案、路标
4. **草图多个变体**: 在纸上或直接在图标编辑器里
5. **在上下文中测试**: 放到软件 mockup 中看实际效果
6. **团队反馈**: 团队给出意见；Steve Jobs 经常给出最终选择
7. **迭代精炼**: "I always like to work with placeholders and tweak and improve images while there's time"
8. **像素级精调**: "You can consider one approach, try another and see how something looks with one dot off or on"

### 4.3 她选择隐喻的方法

> "I got inspiration from all over: books, signage, toys, fine art, folk art — the same as I do now." [一手, TNW 2014]

> "I always go to books when I'm stuck — not all symbol books, but it's good to look at road signs, for example, to get you started." [一手, Stanford 2000]

> "Sometimes people e-mail me now, and say 'How do you learn to design icons?' and 'Are there any good books about learning how to design icons?' I think it's more — you can spread your net wider, and get ideas from all over." [一手, Stanford 2000]

> "You can set out to make a painting, but you can't set out to make a great painting."（关于不要自设太高期望，先开始做）[一手, 转引自 Fast Company/Silberman 前言]

### 4.4 字体设计流程

芝加哥字体的创建过程（New York Magazine 2016）：

1. 约束: 每个大写字母 9 高 x 7 宽
2. 核心规则: 只用水平、垂直、45 度斜线——杜绝锯齿
3. 第一天就完成大写字母和小写字母（"It took about a day"）
4. 最初命名为 "Elefont"（大象字体，因为要粗重）
5. Jobs 要求改名：如果是城市名，必须是世界级城市
6. 最终得名 "Chicago"（源自她和 Hertzfeld 的宾州通勤列车站名系列，被 Jobs 升级为世界城市）

### 4.5 她的迭代哲学

> "I NEVER sketch icons on paper, always directly on the computer." [二手/待验证，来源: 多处设计博客引用，但未找到一手原文出处。可能来自某次采访，但需质疑——与她早期 graph paper 实践明显矛盾。]

**潜在矛盾**: 以上引用（如果确实存在）与她早期用图形纸草图的事实直接冲突。可能的解释：
- (a) 她可能说的是**后来**的实践——当有了工具后，她更喜欢直接在屏幕上工作
- (b) 这句话可能是被二次加工/错误引用，原意可能是"我不满足于只在纸上设计，必须在屏幕上验证"

在 TNW 2014 年她确实说了: "I still think this is great — to see graphics in the software they're destined for as early as possible as opposed to 'on paper' or in art files." ——这更接近她的真实态度：她认为在最终媒介中直接看效果比固守纸上设计更好，但这与"从纸上开始"并不矛盾。

**结论**: "NEVER sketch on paper" 这句话如果存在，很可能指的是她**当工具就绪后**的偏好，而非对图形纸方法的否定。标记为二手/待验证。

## 五、未被充分重视的观点

### 5.1 关于 "Save" 图标的未解问题

> "I still think all the time about what would be a timeless icon for 'save' — not tied to a piece of hardware like a floppy disk or disk drive. Treasure chest? Anchor? Taxidermy? Container?" [一手, KidsCodecs 2020]

这个引言揭示了一个重要的自我批评立场：她认为软盘图标不是一个完美的解决方案（因为它绑定在特定硬件上），40 年后她仍然在思考更好的替代方案。这也呼应了她的隐喻设计哲学——代表功能（存储）而非特定对象（软盘）。

### 5.2 "Verb Problem"（动词问题）

Kare 多次提到抽象动词比具象名词难设计得多：

> "Abstract nouns and verbs are always tough." [一手, VentureBeat 2011]

> "'Undo' remains an unsolved problem (claw hammer pulling out a nail?) along with a number of other perennially tough verbs to symbolize: sort, save, inspire." [一手, Quartz 2019, https://qz.com/1666437/...]

她曾尝试用"猫在镜子里"代表"copycat"来做复制图标，但因为双关不跨语言，放弃。[一手, Stanford 2000]

### 5.3 国际化设计意识（在 1980 年代！）

> "Puns don't translate." [一手, 意思是双关图标不跨文化]

在 1983 年，当大多数软件团队可能还没考虑到国际市场时，Kare 已经在主动避免基于英语双关的图标设计。这来自她的符号学研究背景和对跨文化视觉语言的兴趣。

### 5.4 "不展示太多信息"——从 Steve Jobs 学到的

> "I learned a lot about the cumulative value of attention to detail from Steve [Jobs], and about pushing the limits of a medium. I still think about his philosophy of not showing too much information at once and the value of simplicity in visual messaging." [一手, The COMP Magazine 2021, http://www.thecompmagazine.com/susan-kare-maximizing/]

### 5.5 幽默作为一种设计工具

> "A bit of humor is a good thing!" [一手, KidsCodecs 2020]

> "Probably the happy Macintosh is a logical extension of my tendency to put smiles and faces on almost everything. : n )" [一手, AIGA Eye on Design 2018, https://eyeondesign.aiga.org/the-early-hallmark-cards-of-mac-icon-designer-susan-kare/]

> "I also like the bomb, which is irreverent because I was told it would almost never be seen." [一手, TNW 2014]

Bomb 图标的轶事特别说明问题：她用幽默（卡通炸弹）处理了最负面的用户体验（系统崩溃），把恐惧变成了微笑。有用户真的看到 bomb 后打电话到 Apple 担心电脑会爆炸。[一手, Fast Company/Stanford 2000]

### 5.6 关于 Paul Rand 的自我提问习惯

> "Sometimes when I'm thinking about my own icon work, I read what [Paul Rand] wrote about his work, and about trying to put meaning into what you do." [一手, Stanford 2000]

这暗示她有一个自我检查习惯：在面对设计问题时问自己"Paul Rand 会怎么做？"虽然没有明确说 "WWPRD"，但这内在化了 Rand 的价值观。

### 5.7 "你不需要一本'如何设计图标'的书"——知识来源应该更广

> "Sometimes people e-mail me now, and say 'How do you learn to design icons?' and 'Are there any good books about learning how to design icons?' I think it's more — you can spread your net wider, and get ideas from all over." [一手, Stanford 2000]

她认为图标设计没有专属教材。灵感应该来自美术史、民俗艺术、符号学、工艺、自然——任何地方。

### 5.8 拒绝将"原创性"作为目标

> "Paul Rand said 'Don't try to be original, just try to be good,' which I think is great advice." [一手, Cooper Hewitt 2019]

这与当代设计文化中对"原创性"的崇拜形成鲜明对比。Kare 的作品证明了这个哲学：她设计的最经典的图标（垃圾箱、文件夹、画笔、剪刀）没有一个是"前所未有"的符号，但每个都做到了比之前更好的"good"。

### 5.9 关于 "personality" 和数字化身份——2025 年最新思考

在与 Ledger 的合作中，Kare 提出用"个性"而非字面物品来设计身份徽章，类比 12 星座系统——几亿人只需要 12 个原型。这个思路把她早期的符号学方法扩展到了品牌和身份设计领域。

> "People like knowing... They like finding the symbol that says, this is me." [一手, Ledger 2025]

## 八、方法论提取——为 AI Skill 准备的操作性框架

基于 Kare 本人的言论，可以提取以下可用于构建 AI 图标设计 skill 的原则：

### 8.1 Kare 的"好图标"定义标准
1. **Meaningful**: 图标承载正确的隐喻，不只是好看
2. **Memorable**: 看一次就不忘
3. **Clear**: 瞬间可辨识，无多余细节

### 8.2 Kare 的设计约束优先级
1. 先在概念层面想清楚隐喻（隐喻 > 风格）
2. 限制细节（少 > 多）
3. 选择日常物品作为符号基础
4. 在最终呈现的上下文/尺寸中测试
5. 生产多个变体——不止一个"正确答案"

### 8.3 Kare 的创作激发来源（当卡住时）
1. 符号书（Dreyfuss Symbol Sourcebook）
2. 交通标志
3. 象形文字/kanji
4. 民俗图案/hobo signs
5. 工艺美术（刺绣、马赛克）
6. 美术史（"太阳底下没有新鲜事"）

### 8.4 Kare 的像素工艺原则
1. 每个像素都要有存在的理由
2. 能用更少像素表达的绝不多用
3. 避免锯齿：只用水平、垂直、45 度线
4. 限制被视为自由而非限制
5. "The advantage of working in pixels is that it's iterative — one dot off or on"

### 8.5 Kare 的隐喻选择启发式
1. 代表功能而非特定对象（存储 > 软盘）
2. 选更多人有过经验的日常物（腕表 > 沙漏）
3. 双关不跨文化——避免
4. 抽象度高 = 更普适（McCloud 原则）
5. 名词易、动词难——动词是未解决问题

### 8.6 与 Paul Rand 的对齐
Kare 从 Rand 继承的设计价值观：
- 意义 > 原创性
- 简洁 > 复杂
- 符号应该承载意义而非只是装饰
- "Don't try to be original, just try to be good"
